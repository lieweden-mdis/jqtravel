St. Paul's Cathedral




Tour Detail

Location:1935, Jln Kota, Banda Hilir, 75000 Melaka
Operation Time: Open all day

Description

St. Paul's Cathedral is located on St. Paul's Hill in Malacca City, Malaysia. It is a historic Christian church site. The church was originally built by the Portuguese in 1521 as a Catholic church in honor of St. Paul.

In the early days of the church's construction, it was known as "St. Paul's Church" and was one of the most magnificent churches in Malacca at the time. However, after the Dutch conquered Malacca in 1641, they converted the church into a Dutch Reformed church and renamed it "St Paul's Church".

During Dutch rule, the church's roof was destroyed, leaving only its walls and portico. Despite this, St. Paul's still attracts a large number of tourists and pilgrims. Climbing St. Paul's Hill, visitors can enjoy stunning views of Malacca city while exploring the church ruins and surrounding historical monuments.

The cemetery surrounding St. Paul's Church preserves the tombstones of many important figures, including European colonists and officials. These tombstones provide valuable clues about Malacca's history and demonstrate the city's status as an important colonial city.

Today, St. Paul's Church is one of Malacca's major tourist attractions, attracting a large number of tourists every year. This ancient church ruins is a symbol of Malacca's rich history and cultural heritage, offering visitors a unique historical journey.
