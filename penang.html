Penang
Penang, the "Pearl of the Orient," is a captivating Malaysian state on the northwest coast. Renowned for its rich history, vibrant culture, and mouthwatering cuisine, Penang seamlessly blends old and new. From UNESCO-listed George Town's colonial architecture and colorful street art to pristine beaches and lush hillsides, Penang offers diverse experiences for travelers.

Attractions

Penang Hill
Penang Hill is the highest mountain in Penang. It is the best place to overlook the panoramic view of Penang. This used to be the location of the villas of high-ranking British officials. Soldiers at the foot of the mountain used flag language to convey important messages, hence the name Penang Hill.

Jalan Stesen Bukit Bendera 11500 George Town Penang

Curtis crest tree top walk
This Tree Top Walk is built at an altitude of 820 meters, with a total height of 13 meters. Standing on the Tree Top Walk, you can have a 360-degree bird's-eye view of Penang when the weather is good.

Kek Lok Si Temple
Penang Kek Lok Si Temple is a Buddhist temple located in Air Itam, Penang State, Malaysia. It was built in 1891 and fully completed in 1904. It covers an area of 120,000 square meters. It is the largest and most magnificent Chinese Buddhist temple in Malaysia and the most famous one in Penang. One of the temples.
