Description

Istana Batu, also known as the Stone Palace, is a significant historical landmark located in Kota Bharu, the capital city of Kelantan, Malaysia. Originally built as a royal residence, Istana Batu served as the official palace of the Kelantan Sultanate until 1912. Constructed entirely of wood and stone without the use of nails, the palace showcases traditional Malay architecture and craftsmanship.

Today, Istana Batu has been transformed into the Royal Museum of Kelantan, preserving and showcasing the rich cultural heritage and history of the state. Visitors to the museum can explore various exhibits displaying royal regalia, artifacts, traditional costumes, weaponry, and historical documents. The museum offers insights into the royal lifestyle, customs, and traditions of Kelantanese royalty.

Istana Batu stands as a testament to Kelantan's royal legacy and serves as an important cultural and historical landmark, attracting visitors interested in Malaysia's royal history and traditional architecture.
