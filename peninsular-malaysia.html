Peninsular Malaysia
Peninsular Malaysia, also known as West Malaysia or the “Malaysian Peninsula”, is the western part of Malaysia that comprises the southern part of the Malay Peninsula on Mainland Southeast Asia and the nearby islands. It shares a land border with Thailand to the north and a maritime border with Singapore to the south.  

Famous Attractions

Penang Hill
Penang Hill is the highest mountain in Penang. It is the best place to overlook the panoramic view of Penang. This used to be the location of the villas of high-ranking British officials. Soldiers at the foot of the mountain used flag language to convey important messages, hence the name Penang Hill.

Jalan Stesen Bukit Bendera 11500 George Town Penang
