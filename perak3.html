Sam Poh Tong




Tour Detail

Kampung Gunung Rapat, 31350 Ipoh, Perak

09:00a.m-04:00p.m

Description

Sam Poh Tong is a renowned Buddhist cave temple located near Ipoh, in the state of Perak, Malaysia. It is one of the oldest and most significant cave temples in the country, known for its historical significance, natural beauty, and religious importance.

The name "Sam Poh Tong" translates to "Cave of Three Buddhas" in English. Legend has it that the temple was founded by a Chinese monk who sought refuge in the cave during the Ming Dynasty in the 19th century. Over time, the cave was transformed into a place of worship, and it has since become a popular pilgrimage site for Buddhists and a tourist attraction for visitors from all over the world.

Sam Poh Tong is situated within a limestone cave, which is naturally adorned with stalactites, stalagmites, and other geological formations. The cave's interior is divided into several chambers and halls, each housing statues of Buddha, bodhisattvas, and other deities, as well as altars for prayer and meditation.

One of the main highlights of Sam Poh Tong is its picturesque garden and fish pond, which are located just outside the cave entrance. Visitors can stroll through the garden, feed the fish, and enjoy the tranquil surroundings before or after exploring the temple.

Another notable feature of Sam Poh Tong is the tortoise pond, where visitors can observe and interact with the temple's resident tortoises. According to Buddhist belief, releasing a tortoise into the pond brings good luck and merit.

Additionally, Sam Poh Tong offers a scenic viewpoint from which visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and limestone hills.
