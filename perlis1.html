Description

Gua Kelam, which translates to "Dark Cave" in Malay, is a fascinating limestone cave located near the town of Kaki Bukit in Perlis, Malaysia. It is known for its unique geological formations, historical significance, and natural beauty, making it a popular destination for tourists and outdoor enthusiasts.

The cave is formed from limestone rock and stretches for approximately 370 meters in length. It was naturally carved out by the flow of the nearby Sungai Perlis (Perlis River) over millions of years, resulting in a series of impressive caverns and chambers.

Gua Kelam is accessible via a wooden suspension bridge that spans the Sungai Perlis, offering visitors a thrilling experience as they walk through the dark and narrow passages of the cave. Along the way, visitors can admire the spectacular stalactites and stalagmites that adorn the cave walls and ceilings, formed over centuries by the slow deposition of minerals from dripping water.

One of the highlights of Gua Kelam is the Gua Kelam Recreational Park, which surrounds the cave entrance and features amenities such as picnic areas, walking trails, and camping grounds. The park is a popular spot for outdoor activities such as hiking, birdwatching, and photography, with lush greenery and scenic vistas providing a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Gua Kelam also holds historical significance, as it was once used as a tin mine during the British colonial era in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Remnants of the mining operations, such as rail tracks and mining equipment, can still be seen within the cave, serving as a reminder of its industrial past.
