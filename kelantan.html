Kelantan
Kelantan, located in the northeastern region of Malaysia, is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life. It is often referred to as the "cradle of Malay culture" due to its preservation of customs, language, and arts. With its beautiful mosques, scenic rivers, and lush jungles, Kelantan offers a glimpse into Malaysia's diverse and colorful tapestry.

Attractions

Istana Batu
Istana Batu, also known as the Stone Palace, is a significant historical landmark located in Kota Bharu, the capital city of Kelantan, Malaysia. Originally built as a royal residence, Istana Batu served as the official palace of the Kelantan Sultanate until 1912. Constructed entirely of wood and stone without the use of nails, the palace showcases traditional Malay architecture and craftsmanship.......

Pantai Cahaya Bulan
Pantai Cahaya Bulan, often referred to as PCB or Moonlight Beach, is one of the most popular beaches in Kelantan, Malaysia. Located just a short drive from Kota Bharu, the capital city, PCB stretches along the coast of the South China Sea, offering stunning views of the ocean and sandy shores.The beach is renowned for its tranquil atmosphere and natural beauty........

Kelantan State Museum
The Kelantan State Museum, located in Kota Bharu, the capital city of Kelantan, Malaysia, is a prominent cultural institution dedicated to preserving and showcasing the rich heritage and history of the state. Established in 1992, the museum occupies a modern building and offers a comprehensive insight into Kelantan's diverse cultural identity, traditions, and arts........
